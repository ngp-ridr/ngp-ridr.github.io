## Who We're Looking For

NGP RIDR is a community-engaged initiative. Our solutions are co-designed with the people and organizations who use drought information — not handed down to them. We're actively seeking engagement from:

- **State and federal agency staff** involved in drought assessment, water management, or agricultural programs
- **Tribal nations and tribal organizations** with interests in drought monitoring, data sovereignty, or workforce development
- **Agricultural producers, ranchers, and commodity groups** whose operations are affected by drought classification and USDA program triggers
- **Insurance and risk professionals** working in agricultural or natural resource sectors
- **Researchers** working on drought science, hydrology, machine learning, or related fields
- **Extension educators and outreach specialists** connecting science to producers and land managers

## How to Engage

### Listening Sessions and Workshops

Throughout 2026, NGP RIDR solution teams are hosting stakeholder listening sessions and working sessions to gather input on challenges, priorities, and potential solutions. These build on existing forums where possible — we're bringing RIDR conversations into spaces where partners already gather.

Upcoming engagements include:

- **National Coordinated Soil Moisture Monitoring Network (NCSMN) Forum** — June 9–11, 2026
- **American Association of State Climatologists (AASC) Annual Meeting** — June 24–26, 2026
- **FALCON Workshop** — November 6–8, 2026 · Kansas City, MO

[Contact us](/contact/) to learn more about attending or contributing to a session.

### Partner Organizations

If your organization works in drought assessment, water resources, agricultural risk, or tribal environmental management and wants to be part of the NGP RIDR network, reach out. Formal partnership involves:

- Participation in relevant solution working groups
- Input into indicator selection, governance design, and dashboard development
- Opportunities to shape the Phase II implementation proposal

### Stay Informed

Sign up for NGP RIDR updates to receive news about workshops, publications, and program milestones as they happen.

[Contact us to join the mailing list](/contact/)

## Timeline

NGP RIDR Phase I runs through January 2027, with a focus on stakeholder engagement, co-production, and building the foundation for a Phase II implementation proposal. Here's where we are:

| Phase | Period | Focus |
|---|---|---|
| **Phase 1 — Information Gathering** | Feb 2026 – Jan 2027 | Listening sessions, working groups, solution refinement |
| **Phase 2 — Synthesis** | Jan 2027 | In-person leadership meeting; synthesis across all five solutions |
| **Phase 3 — Proposal Development** | Mar – Sep 2027 | Writing and review of Phase II implementation proposal |

Phase II (if funded) will provide up to $15 million over five years to deploy the regional drought assessment system at scale.
